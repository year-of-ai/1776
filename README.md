## Notable Events of 1776

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| [Declaration of Independence](politics/declaration-of-independence.md) | The Second Continental Congress adopted the U.S. Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776, founding a new nation. |
| Thomas Paine's *Common Sense* | Paine's influential January 1776 pamphlet galvanized colonial support for independence from Britain. |
| Battle of Long Island | British forces defeated Washington's Continental Army in August 1776, the largest battle of the Revolutionary War. |
| [Battle of Trenton](military/battle-of-trenton.md) | Washington's surprise crossing of the Delaware River on December 26, 1776 reversed Continental Army fortunes. |
| [Adam Smith publishes *The Wealth of Nations*](economics/wealth-of-nations.md) | Smith's foundational work of modern economics was published March 9, 1776, defining free-market principles. |
| Edward Gibbon's *Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire* | The first volume of Gibbon's landmark historical work was published in February 1776. |
| Captain Cook's Third Voyage departs | HMS Resolution and HMS Discovery left Plymouth in July 1776 on Cook's final Pacific expedition. |
| Virginia Declaration of Rights | George Mason's Virginia Declaration of Rights, adopted June 12, 1776, directly influenced the U.S. Bill of Rights. |
| Mozart composes *Serenata notturna* | Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart composed the Serenata notturna (K. 239) in January 1776 in Salzburg. |
